Description of Animals

These animals are all endangered at different levels of severity. Look below to learn more about these beautiful animals!

Amur Leopard
Amur Leopards live in temperate climates, and live in solitary. When they hunt for food, they usually keep it to themselves. Amur Leopards are victim to the illegal wild life trade, and are killed for their fur.
Javan Rhino
The Javan Rhino lives in rainy, warm grasslands. They live mostly in solitary, but deviate awawy from that lifestly due to courtship and offspring-rearing. Rhinos have had a history of being hunted. During colonial times, they were killed by trophy hunters. Poaching is still prevalent as well.
Asian Elephant
Asian Elephants are very social animals, that form moderately sized groups. They are also an important cultural icon in Asia, especially in Hindu mythology. Asian Elephants are endangered due to poaching and habitat loss.
Lemur
Lemurs get their name from the latin word, Lemures, which means 'spirit of the night'. The largest every living type of lemur was the Archaeoindris.
